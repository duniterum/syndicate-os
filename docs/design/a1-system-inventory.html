The one insight

The Protocol side is already live, and every “My” figure already comes back from the server. A1 is mostly a surface to lay over data you already have — not a new machine. That’s why the honest first slice needs zero new indexing.

Already live today

most of the machine exists
  • LIVE
    The public heartbeat — /activity
    The complete protocol history: seats, burns, liquidity, archive mints, treasury moves, milestones, era turns, footprint rises, Chronicle promotions — every line verify-linked. 12 filter chips. This is the “Protocol” half ready to go.
  • LIVE
    The dashboard pulse (5 lines)
    The 5 newest protocol lines on your Member Home. Its own code says: “when A1 lands, this zone gains the My | Protocol lens.”
  • LIVE
    The full receipt ticket
    The solemn on-chain receipt — seat, “where your money went”, QR verify, save-as-PDF, share card. It exists — but only for a flash at checkout; a past purchase can’t be reopened.
  • LIVE
    All 5 of your own data feeds
    Your purchases (as rows) · your seat + founding receipt · your footprint + 12-rung ladder · your introductions (counts) · your inbox — all already served, own-row only, behind the sign-in wall.

What’s missing — your “My” side

the surface, not the data
  • LOCKED
    The receipt binder
    The “Receipts” door is visible but locked. Your receipts are never gathered — you can see the count (“6 confirmed”) but can’t browse or reopen them.
  • NOT BUILT
    The My | Protocol pulse lens
    No toggle to flip the pulse to your own events. The data to build it (your rows) is already live.
  • NOT BUILT
    Per-row histories (M10)
    Your introductions are a total only — never itemised (“who, when”). Same for per-receipt / per-commission rows.
  • CAPPED
    “Your recent activity” stops at 5
    Rows 6+ are already fetched but never shown — there’s no “view all”. Lifting the cap is nearly free.
  • GAP
    The V2 “Routed” proof fold
    Some older-engine tickets don’t print their money-flow proof block — the paired on-chain event isn’t folded in yet.

The mirror rule. Harvest the origin’s completeness and ambition — never its baggage. Leave behind: the read-only-era hedging (“pending / not portable / candidate, not official”), the XP/level gain-framing, and the email reflex. Today’s system is more advanced; we take what it had more of, not what held it back. (And “Since you were away” is retired — it lives in the notification centre now; we do not rebuild it.)
The decision — you pick from the whole picture

The scope ladder for A1

Each rung adds to the one before. The green tag = client-only, zero new indexing (rides a normal deploy). The amber tag = needs a small server change or its own slice.

  1. S0
    Receipt binder client-only recommended start
    Your purchases as browsable rows, each with a “view receipt” that reopens the ticket we already built — and the Receipts door goes live. Lifts the 5-row cap. Zero new backend.
  2. S1
    + My | Protocol pulse lens client-only recommended
    A toggle on the pulse: Protocol (live now) vs Mine (your own events). The data’s already there.
  3. S2
    + Per-row histories — M10 client-only recommended
    Your introductions / receipts / commissions itemised, verify-linked per row. Closes the last “coming soon” cards on your page.
  4. S3
    + Receipt roadmap small server
    A public /receipt/{tx} page, the living ticket + share card, and the V2 “Routed” proof fold. Its own slice.
  5. S4
    + Auto-generator own slice
    Personal events pushed as they happen — “your referral succeeded”, “commission paid $X”, “you rose to Patron”. Sets up the FIRSTS engine → My Chronicle next.
My recommendation: ship S0 + S1 + S2 as “A1, thin version” — all three are client-only, zero new indexing, and they’re exactly the finish-order definition of A1. S3 and S4 are their own later slices (each touches the server or is its own machine) — we don’t fold them in silently.
