## §1 · THE RECEIPT SYSTEM WE HAVE

### The ticket itself — a designed paper document, not a data table

When a member buys a seat at /join and the purchase confirms on-chain, the protocol prints **THE TICKET**: a narrow paper receipt (till-receipt silhouette, 340px wide) with seven zones separated by dashed perforation-style rules, top to bottom:

1. **The identity head** — the Syndicate's gold mark (the issuer's seal, your correction of 2026-07-17), "THE SYNDICATE / ON-CHAIN MEMBERSHIP PROTOCOL", the document title "Membership receipt — proof of purchase", the chain line, and the **gold chapter chip** ("Chapter I · Genesis Signal").
2. **When and where** — date, block number, era.
3. **The seat** — Seat #N emphasized, holder in short form, SYN received with the rate, and "brought by 0x…" when a referrer introduced the buyer.
4. **The commerce block** — "Membership — Seat #7 … $5.00", then **TOTAL PAID** in the ticket's loudest type.
5. **"WHERE YOUR MONEY WENT — in your own transaction"** (gold title) — "Referral · paid first $0.25" when there was one, then the plain-words lead ("The remaining $4.75:") and the three indented split lines: Vault · 70%, Liquidity · 20%, Operations · 10%. The lines **sum exactly** on screen because amounts are printed exactly, never rounded.
6. **The living zone** — the story coordinate ("Seat #7 of Chapter I · Era 2"), the optional gold witness line, and the doctrine quote in a gold-bordered blockquote.
7. **The verify zone** — "ONE WALLET · ONE SEAT", a **QR code of the transaction** on a white chip (scannable even in dark theme), "SCAN TO VERIFY", "DON'T TRUST — VERIFY", and "the transaction hash *is* the signature."

Below the paper: **THE ONE DOOR** (exactly one gold next-step link, chosen from the member's real situation — the Archive or their referral link) and the action bar (Copy link · Share · **Save image** · Verify), which stays off any print or saved image. Printing the page gives a clean save-as-PDF of the ticket alone, in light colors.

### The laws the ticket lives by (in plain words)

- **Born only from a sealed transaction.** No preview, partial, or pending ticket can even be expressed — the design refuses.
- **Every figure is the chain's own number, verbatim.** The ticket never does math on money; the 70/20/10 percentages exist only as label text. (The pre-purchase money path on /join DOES compute — that is legitimate because a quote is future money; a receipt is past money, an accounting document.)
- **Historical honesty.** The ticket covers every purchase ever made, across all four generations of the sale machinery. Where an old purchase didn't record something (era, rate, splits), the ticket shows absence — it never invents "Era 1".
- **No selling on a receipt.** No discounts, urgency, rewards vocabulary — a ticket proves a purchase, it never sells.
- **Readability first.** No tiny text, no italics, values never truncate — they wrap.

### The 83 guard pins

An automated guard runs on every build with **83 individual checks** that make these laws unbreakable by future code: the banned sales/urgency vocabulary, the exact buyer's-tongue titles ("TOTAL PAID", "WHERE YOUR MONEY WENT" — and the killed title "routing proof" can never return), zero client math on any amount, exact formatting, real-transactions-only, one door max, the readability laws, one single place in the whole app that builds a referral link, print purity, the share card's size and weight ceiling, and the engraved roadmap itself.

### Where it renders today — and the engraved future

The ticket renders in **exactly one place**: the checkout success moment on /join. It is **transient** — close the page and it's gone; it cannot be reopened. The member menu already shows a locked **"Receipts"** door promising the binder ("Your receipts in one binder… the binder surface is coming"). The roadmap engraved into the receipt system's own header, protected by a guard pin: checkout ticket (done) → a **public receipt page per transaction** with a proper PDF → the **LIVING TICKET** (a receipt that re-reads the record and gains earned lines over time) → receipt-as-artifact.

One more thing the system anticipates by design: the receipt spine is **kind-extensible** — it was built so new kinds of receipts (an artifact purchase, a commission) join the ONE spine rather than spawning parallel receipt models. This matters directly in §4.

## §4 · TODAY'S GAP — why the Commissions anatomy card feels poor

### What's on the Commissions tab now
A generic dashboard card: a small mono label ("The anatomy of one commission — your latest receipt"), a "Live · your own row" pill, then **six flat label/value rows** — The buyer paid $5.00 / Your commission 5% $0.25 / Sent to the Syndicate $4.75 / Vault 70% · Liquidity 20% · Operations 10% — a date, and a small "verify" link.

**Its data and words are right.** It shows your real latest commission, the figures are the chain's own and are checked server-side so the split sums exactly, and the vocabulary follows canon ("commission", "Sent to the Syndicate").

**Its design is not.** It has none of the house receipt language that exists one module away: no paper silhouette, no dashed zone rules, no gold identity head or mark, no chapter chip, no big-figure emphasis, no QR, no doctrine line, no "ONE WALLET · ONE SEAT / DON'T TRUST — VERIFY", no share, no save, no print. The protocol already speaks a rich, solemn receipt language — and the introducer's view of the very same transaction speaks in plain dashboard prose. That's the rejection, precisely.

Put another way: one transaction has **three tellings**. The buyer sees it before purchase (the money path on /join — designed). The buyer sees it after purchase (the ticket — the most designed surface we own). The **introducer** sees it on /referral — and that third telling is the only one without the house design.

### Every piece available to close the gap
- **The design language**: the full ticket component, the receipt spine (built to be extended with new receipt kinds — a commission receipt is exactly the anticipated path, never a parallel model), the print/PDF projection, the chapter canon, the guard (extensible with new pins).
- **The share machinery**: the proven 1200×630 card engine with its weight ceiling and native-share path, the QR block, the share menu, the one link resolver.
- **The data — already served, per row, today**: for every introduction the server sends the member the day it sealed, who joined (short form, per settled identity law), whether that member's seat is still durable, the commission amount, the transaction anchor with its explorer link, the block — and since the latest slice, the **full money anatomy of each row**: what the buyer paid, the commission rate and amount, what went to the Syndicate, and the Vault/Liquidity/Operations amounts, each verified internally consistent before it's ever served. **The per-row raw material for a real designed receipt is on the wire right now.**
- **Not yet served per row** (all present in the underlying record; adding any is a small, careful server amendment): the introduced member's seat number, the era, the SYN amount, the exact time of day (we serve the day). A commission ticket can be honest and complete without them — absence renders as absence, the same law the buyer's ticket already follows for old purchases.

## §5 · THE SCOPE MENU — pick the size of the slice

All options obey the standing laws: wireframe before any layout change, preview gate before any commit, SEO rides the slice, and the link hero stays the one place a bare link lives.

Latest ticket open, earlier ones as a list that opens the same ticket.
*Reuses*: the receipt spine (adds a "commission" kind — the anticipated extension), the ticket's visual system, the guard (new pins). *Adds*: the commission receipt model + its render variant, wireframe, guard pins. *Weight*: one slice, client-only, no server change. **DEPLOY** (visible page change).

**B — A + the shareable commission card.** Everything in A, plus a Save image / Share action on the commission ticket, producing a 1200×630 card from the proven engine: your introduction sealed on-chain, the commission figure (Visibility Law), verify line, and the QR of your own introduction link — every shared receipt recruits.
*Reuses*: the entire receipt-share-card engine, the native share path, the one link resolver. *Adds*: a commission variant of the card + guard pins. *Weight*: A + roughly half a slice, still client-only. **DEPLOY**.

**C — The Referrer Kit v1 (the queued M2 slice).** The one-click **member standing card**: seat · chapter · verified introductions · standing, generated as a living image + link + QR, mounted on /referral (the mockup's unimplemented "Referrer kit" button, made real; the unmounted introducer card retired or absorbed). This is the origin's member-card craft harvested into our engine — chain-proven figures only.
*Reuses*: the share engine, QR, link resolver, own-row data already served. *Adds*: the card design (wireframe required), the one-click flow, guard pins. *Weight*: its own slice; combinable with A/B or standalone. **DEPLOY**.

**D — The receipt binder + the public receipt page.** The engraved next step of the roadmap: the locked "Receipts" door opens — every past purchase reopenable as its full ticket (list → detail), plus the public per-transaction receipt page with a proper PDF, so a receipt is never transient again. The five honesty invariants of the binder canon apply at first build.
*Reuses*: the ticket end-to-end, the purchase-history data already served, the print projection. *Adds*: the binder surface, the public page + its metadata, PDF path, possibly small server additions. *Weight*: the heaviest — a short arc (2–3 slices), not one. **DEPLOY**, likely staged.

**E — Living link previews (the origin's crown jewel, harvested).** Pages stop unfurling with one static image: generated preview cards per page — and later per seat — stamped "as of block N", honest states, premium X fallback. The origin ranked the grey empty card its single biggest leak; ours is the same today.
*Reuses*: the origin's proven template design + our indexer (their slow plumbing stays out). *Adds*: server-side image generation — the "real infra" the master brief deferred to v2. *Weight*: server work, its own arc. **DEPLOY** with server changes.

**The honest ordering note**: A (or A+B) closes today's rejection directly and touches nothing on the server. C is the queued growth artifact. D fulfills the promise already written on the member menu. E is the compounding shareability layer underneath all of them. They stack — nothing in A/B is thrown away by C, D, or E.
