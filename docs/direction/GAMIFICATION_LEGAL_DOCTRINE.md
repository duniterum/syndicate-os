## The real red line — it is NOT "spendable"

A common mistake: banning "spendable points." That is wrong. Major programs all have
**spendable** points that stay perfectly legal. The real line is:

> **cash value · transferability · financial return.**

"Spendable inside a closed loop for non-financial perks" is safe. "Convertible to cash /
transferable / yielding a return" is what turns points into money, a token, or a security.

## The universal closed-loop structure (near-identical wording everywhere)

Every compliant program carries this language. It is what keeps points from being money,
a security, or (usually) taxable income:

1. **"Points have no cash value."**
2. **"Points are not redeemable for cash."**
3. **"Points are not transferable, saleable, or assignable."**
4. **"Points are not property; you have no vested right or interest in them."**
5. **"Points remain the property of the Company."**
6. **"The Company may modify, suspend, or terminate the program at any time."**
7. **Redeemable ONLY for defined perks** (discounts, products, access) — a closed loop,
   never an open currency.
8. Forfeiture on inactivity / account closure; one human account, no bots.

## Rules for The Syndicate (stricter, because we are a token protocol)

Recognition / points MAY exist, and MAY even be "spendable", ONLY IF:

- ✅ **Closed loop, non-financial perks only** — access, cosmetics, recognition, status,
  standing — plus the full wording above.
- ❌ **Never convertible** to SYN, crypto, cash, or anything with market value.
- ❌ **Never transferable / tradeable** — otherwise it is a de-facto token.
- ❌ **Never a yield / return / financial advantage** — otherwise it is a security.

**Crypto wrinkle:** because SYN is a token, regulators scrutinize any financial advantage to
holders. Keep recognition points **off-chain or explicitly non-transferable**, so they are
never confused with a tradeable token. Recognition is multi-axis (Capital / Builder /
Connector / Operator / Verifier / Historian / Steward…) and is **status, not money.**

## Reference: what to copy vs. avoid

- **Supa "Reward Points" (correctly structured):** copies loyalty language verbatim —
  *"Points are NOT money, crypto, or tokens … no cash value … a loyalty mechanism, not a
  financial instrument."* This closed-loop model is the **safe template**.
- **AVOID:** Supa's XP→USDC seasons (points redeemable for real money) and entity-chain's
  APY / staking-yield / profit-sharing / referral-% bonuses — all financial-return framing,
  banned by our doctrine.
